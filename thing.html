The Thing(1982)

The ultimate of alien terror, John Carpanter's 1982 critically panned to critically revered movie in the years since it's home video release. A remake of the Howard Hawk's classic, 12 people in a US station in Antartcica are put in the mits of a alien with the ability to absord and take the form of their victim. The paranoia sets in as the close encounter with the "Thing" kicks things into high gear in this gloomy and dramatic remake. Instead of the glorious defeat of the alien in the orginal movie, Carpanter takes a more gloomy and distressed appoarch to the story. The clasterphobic setting of the artic puts the team into immediate mistrust as the thing comes into the station through a very different form. The night is filled with blue lights that highlight the shadows of those that need to step outside the in below freezing weather. It speaks alot higher to the idea about how people would react in such a odd,but horrifying scenario. As the fellow man with you could also be the most dangerous enemy. The movie would universally hated because of other alien films that demostrated them as cutsy and loveable. From then on, the movie would receive the vaildation that it deserved as a apex of horror and another horror classic.
