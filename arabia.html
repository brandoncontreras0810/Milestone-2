Lawrence of Arabia(1962)

The semi-biopic of T.E Lawrence and his time in Arabia with a tribe that are bent to take over a part of the country from the Turkish army. Lawrence is set as an above average man with a certain charm that wins the favor of Prince Feisal. Leading an army to take over parts of turkish occupied Arabia. With the stilling images of the beauty that director David Lean has put in his desert shots, this is a staple and apex of cinematic story telling and cinemtography. A adaption of the book "The Seven Pillars of Wisdom" by the real T.E Lawrence, the story follows a linear structure that starts with the death of T.E Lawrence. More often than not, Lawrence is shown to be very eccentric and charismatic to the point that you can see that he's very different from the men around him. He's sent on a mission in Arabia to which leads to the take over parts of Arabia from the Turkish to till the end, establish the United Arabs. Lawrence goes through an arc in the film that he finds the violent actions he takes as enjoyable, but is visablly traumatic from his first kill and his trip to his station in Egypt. Lawrence of Arabia tells a very epic story as the vast deserts that have are spilled with blood that will never be seen, but told.
