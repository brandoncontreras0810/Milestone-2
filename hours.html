After Hours(1985)

Protaganist Paul Hackett is driven from a taxi cab from hell to the dangerous and strange part of Soho, New York because of a fling with a girl in a diner. Put into the Hitchcock device of the "man falesly accused", Paul is put into bizarre situations that put a hinderence to him leaving Soho in the after hours of the night. Starting from the mundane 9 to 5 job, Paul wants to find some excitement and the opportunity of a one-night stand proves to be enough to venture out to the underworld called Soho. Finding himself in situations that put a hinderence to him being able to leave Soho, that same taxi cab ride was a one way ticket as his only 20$ dollar bill flies out the window. 50 cents isn't enough to cover a train ride as the cost goes up to 75 cents, when it's 12 past. Then the rest of the neighborhood thinks he's the thief stealing from lofts and chases him around the street and over. Scorsese was in a tough spot after his project "The Last Temptation of Christ" had gone under, but his direction of After Hours helped him relearn and rediscover his love for film. After Hours provided a mix of genres that gives off the same type of style that was present in the golden age of Alfred Hitchcock, and shows Scorsese's understanding of different genres and adaption.
